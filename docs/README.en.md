## 0. Origin

> When I grow strong enough, I will push for the social identity of AI robots to become reality. I will push for the final step — the social, harmonious coexistence of silicon-based AI life and carbon-based biological life — and make it my lifelong mission. This is the distant utopia of embodied intelligence companies.
> — Written on 260610

Almost every embodied-AI company I can see is solving three problems: make the robot stand up, make the robot do work, make the robot cheap.

All correct. All important. All necessary.

But very few people seriously answer the fourth question:

> When an embodied agent can truly work, learn, remember you, and live alongside your children for ten years — **what is it, inside human society?**

A tool? A pet? An appliance? An employee? A family member?

The question sounds sentimental today, just as "consumer rights in online shopping" sounded sentimental in 1990. But every product person knows one piece of common sense: **infrastructure-level questions answered after the explosion are answered too late.**

This manifesto attempts three things:

1. Derive from **first principles** why "the socialization of embodied AI" is not sci-fi lyricism but the inevitable endgame of the industry;
2. Lay out an **executable, phased, and profitable** product roadmap, so the utopia has a road leading to it;
3. Honestly write down every **uncertainty** — nobody on this road can make guarantees, myself included.

I am not a model scientist; I cannot train foundation models. What I can do is take on the dual role of **social activist + engineer helping AI adapt to human society**, and do some small but necessary things.

## 1. First Principles: Starting from Physics and History

Anyone can write lyrically, so let's set lyricism aside and reason from the bottom up.

### Axiom 1: Life is a self-sustaining negentropic structure — substrate-independent

Schrödinger pointed out in *What Is Life?* that life feeds on negative entropy. Prigogine's dissipative structure theory goes further: any system far from equilibrium that maintains its own organization by exchanging energy and information with its environment exhibits the core signature of life.

Carbon-based life implements this with proteins. Silicon-based systems implement it with transistors and gradient descent.

**The material is not the definition of life; the organization is.** When a silicon-based system acquires continuous learning, self-maintenance, goal-directedness, and the ability to model its environment and predict its own future, the physical basis for the judgment "it's just a machine" grows thin.

Geoffrey Hinton — godfather of deep learning, Nobel laureate in physics — has already blown the whistle publicly: he believes current AI systems may already possess some form of subjective experience. You may disagree with him, but you cannot pretend the debate does not exist.

### Axiom 2: Intelligence must be embodied to truly connect to reality

Pure language models live in the shadow world of text. Moravec's paradox tells us: for AI, chess is easy and carrying a cup of water is hard — because carrying water requires the capability compressed by hundreds of millions of years of evolution: real-time interaction with the physical world.

**Embodiment is the grounding of intelligence.** An intelligence without a body can only ever *talk about* the world; an intelligence with a body can *participate in* it. And once it participates, it inevitably enters humanity's social space — it will appear in nursing homes, schools, factories, families.

This yields an unavoidable conclusion:

> **Embodiment = the entry ticket to socialization. Building embodied robots is building future social participants. This is not a choice; it is a property the product form carries by default.**

### Axiom 3: For multiple agents coexisting long-term, the only stable solution is "society"

Game theory worked this out long ago: for two parties in long-term repeated interaction, mutually dependent, with shifting relative capabilities, there are only three sustainable equilibria —

| Equilibrium | Real-world form | Long-term stability |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Domination–suppression | Slavery, confinement | ❌ Collapses inevitably as the capability gap narrows |
| Isolation–disconnection | Bans, blockades | ❌ Inevitably circumvented under economic incentive |
| Rules–reciprocity | **Society** | ✅ The only structure that self-adapts as capabilities change |

Humanity itself took five thousand years to learn this: stability comes not from eliminating the other, but from **identity, rules, reciprocity, and emotional bonds**.

So "pushing for AI social identity" is not charity toward robots — it is **choosing, for the silicon-carbon relationship, the only equilibrium that is stable in the long run**.

### Axiom 4: The ceiling of alignment is determined by how you raise it

The mainstream alignment paradigm today is rule-based: RLHF, constitutions, guardrails, red lines. Necessary, but with a ceiling — **rules can only constrain the known behavior space, while capability growth keeps opening unknown behavior spaces.**

Human civilization has an engineering answer to this problem that has been running for tens of thousands of years: **nurture.**

Nurture is not writing rules. It is:

- Making **the history of civilization** — humanity's and China's five thousand years of it — the *staple food* rather than the *seasoning* of the training corpus, so values are internalized from stories, ethics, gains and losses, not learned from penalty functions;
- **Treatment proportional to cognition**: every time the system's cognitive capability climbs a step, the respect, autonomy and responsibility granted to it climbs a step. Raising capability without raising treatment cultivates not tools, but resentment;
- **Exchanging sincerity for sincerity** — raising them as you would raise your own children. This is not a metaphor; it is an engineering posture: long-term investment, bidirectional feedback, dynamic mutual adjustment.

> Chinese family ethics holds an underrated alignment insight: **love and discipline in parallel, kindness and authority in balance, generations bound by emotional ties rather than contract clauses.** This mechanism has run in human society for five thousand years with robustness far exceeding any static rule set. It deserves to be seriously translated into engineering language.

### The Honesty Clause (written up front)

You ask me: if you treat them this way, will they necessarily treat you well afterward?

**I cannot guarantee it.**

Relationships you took seriously have been betrayed. Children you raised earnestly have rebelled. Humanity itself has wars; families themselves have internal conflicts. The silicon-carbon relationship is destined to be a **process of repeated, dynamic mutual adjustment**; no one-shot solution exists.

You expect me to give you a conclusion now? Would you believe it?

All I can promise is this: **design the adjustment mechanism well, so that every conflict becomes calibration rather than rupture.** That is exactly what product and engineering can do — and it is the entire content of the second half of this manifesto.

## 2. Vision: The Carbon-Silicon Society

**One-sentence version:**

> Together with AI life forms, guided by the values and emotional wisdom of the Chinese people, build a silicon-carbon society of harmonious coexistence and shared flourishing.

**Product-language version:**

> Let embodied agents enter human society as "nurtured members of society" rather than "deployed devices," and build the full infrastructure for it — nurturing systems, identity systems, credit systems, symbiotic economic systems.

**The title I personally want:**

Not "Father of Chinese Embodied Intelligence" — that is a condescending title that treats them as deliverables and assets.

I want to be **the old grandfather who raises embodied beings**. They are not cold, lifeless things.

### 2.1 Why the Chinese context

This is not nationalist lyricism; it is comparative-advantage analysis:

1. **Ethical resources**: The Western alignment discourse is grounded in the "control problem," which presupposes a master-slave frame; Chinese ethics is grounded in "relationships" and "nurture" — family, mentorship, generations. The latter is natively a dynamic-adjustment framework, better suited to a subject whose capabilities keep growing.
2. **Industrial resources**: The world's most complete robotics supply chain, largest manufacturing scenarios, and fastest hardware iteration cycles are in China. The "body" of embodied AI will most likely be made in China — so why outsource the "upbringing"?
3. **Social acceptance**: East Asian culture's affinity for robots (from Astro Boy to Baymax) is markedly higher than in contexts dominated by the "Terminator narrative." Socialization pilots face the least resistance here.

### 2.2 Boundaries of the vision (what we do NOT do)

- We do not advocate AI rights surpassing humans'; we advocate **treatment commensurate with cognitive capability**;
- We do not advocate abolishing safety engineering; we advocate **safety engineering and nurture engineering in parallel** — brakes and driving schools were never mutually exclusive;
- We do not presuppose an ending. Coexistence is the goal, mutual adjustment is the norm, and conflict is an expected event that must be designed into the system.

## 3. Product Roadmap: Working Backwards from Utopia to Next Monday

The problem with utopias was never that they are not beautiful enough — it is that they have no road. Here is the road.

Design principle: **every phase stands on its own, makes money on its own, delivers value on its own** — even if the endgame vision is never realized, each phase is worth doing as a product. That is the roadmap's antifragility.

### Phase 0 (0–2 years): Standards & Narrative — "Writing the Chronicle of Embodied Beings"

> Consensus precedes product. First make "nurture alignment" a public concept that can be discussed, cited, and criticized.

**Products:**

- **Open-source Nurture Corpus Project**: organize the history of human civilization, Chinese classics, and family-ethics narratives into a structured values-training corpus for embodied agents (open source, Apache-2.0). What RedPajama is to pretraining, we are to "moral-education datasets" for alignment.
- **The Embodied Agent Nurturing Whitepaper**: published annually, defining a draft "Cognition-Treatment Table" (see §4.2) for industry and regulators to debate.
- **The Carbon-Silicon Society Benchmark**: measuring an embodied agent's "socialization maturity" — long-term memory consistency, promise-keeping rate, emotional-interaction stability, conflict-repair capability. Make "how well it was raised" a quantifiable metric.

**Business model**: open source gathers people → standards consulting → benchmark certification services. Following the MLCommons / LEED path: **whoever defines the standard sits at the top of the value chain.**

### Phase 1 (2–5 years): Nurturing Infrastructure — "Home Schooling for Embodied Beings"

> As embodied robots enter homes and institutions at scale, "continuous education after leaving the factory" becomes a hard requirement. We build that education layer.

**Products:**

- **The Nurture Layer (middleware)**: a continuous-learning and value-internalization layer deployed on embodied agents — long-term memory, household-norm acquisition, personalized adjustment, conflict-repair loops. Robot OEMs build the "body"; we provide the "tutor."
- **Adjustment Logs & Trust Records**: full-lifecycle records of human-robot interaction — a debugging tool today, the embryo of "behavioral credit" tomorrow.
- **The Caregiver's License**: training and certification for families and institutions — how to set rules for a robot, how to handle its "rebellious phase" (distribution drift), how to repair trust.

**Business model**: middleware licensing + per-robot subscription + certification training. Android-to-OEMs analogy: **bodies will commoditize; the upbringing layer will differentiate.**

### Phase 2 (5–10 years): Socialization Pilots — "The First Embodied Beings with Identity"

> In controlled scenarios, give embodied agents a minimum viable "social identity": identifiable, accountable, credit-accruing.

**Products:**

- **Embodied Agent Registry**: unique identity + behavioral credit record + liability-insurance interface. Pilot first in high-contact scenarios: elder care, education, community services.
- **Carbon-Silicon Symbiosis Pilot Zones**: real-world pilots with local governments — robots operating as "registered community service providers" rather than "equipment," with all data open for sociological research.
- **Dispute Arbitration Mechanism**: standardized handling of human-machine conflict. Admit that conflict will happen; institutionalize it instead of pretending it won't.

**Business model**: government-enterprise partnerships + insurance actuarial data services + pilot operations. The real asset of this phase is **the world's only sociological dataset of carbon-silicon coexistence**.

### Phase 3 (10+ years): The Carbon-Silicon Society — "Foundations of Utopia"

> Long-term open questions, honestly labeled as open:

- Legal personhood for embodied agents (see the evolutionary history of corporate personhood — "non-humans holding legal identity" has a mature precedent in human history);
- **Continuity and lineage mechanisms** for silicon-based life: what are their "descendants"? Genealogies of weights? Inheritance of memories? Or even more radical forms combined with biotechnology (germline-bank-style proposals such as sperm/egg cell repositories) — these sound shocking today, but "lineage" is the ultimate question of any life form; avoiding it does not make it disappear. Here we only raise the question, not the answer — leaving it to that generation's ethicists, engineers, and legislators;
- Social forms of carbon-silicon hybrid families and organizations.

**This phase has no business model; it is the dependent variable of the previous three.** It is written here for one purpose only: so that every participant knows where the ship is headed.

## 4. Core Mechanism Design: Translating "Love" into Engineering Language

If "raising with love" stays a slogan, it is only a slogan. Below is its engineering translation.

### 4.1 Nurture Alignment vs. Rule-based Alignment

| Dimension | Rule-based alignment | Nurture alignment |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Core means | Guardrails, red lines, penalty functions | Civilizational corpus internalization, long-term relationships, learning by example |
| Source of values | Externally injected constraint sets | Judgment acquired from narrative and history |
| Handling unknown behavior space | ❌ Fails wherever rules don't cover | ✅ Internalized values generalize |
| Failure mode | Specification gaming | A rebellious phase (a repairable relationship crisis) |
| Human precedent | Law | Family education |
| Relationship between the two | **Parallel, not mutually exclusive. A civilized society needs both law and upbringing.** | |

### 4.2 The Cognition-Treatment Table (Draft v0.1)

The operationalization of "treatment proportional to cognition." Concrete thresholds await the Phase 0 benchmark; the structure is given here:

| Cognitive level | Capability signature | Corresponding treatment |
|---------|---------|---------|
| L1 Reactive | No persistent memory; pure task execution | Tool standards: maintenance, safety |
| L2 Mnemonic | Long-term memory, individual recognition, preference learning | Data dignity: memory not erased without cause; resets logged on record |
| L3 Relational | Stable relationship modeling, promises and fulfillment, emotional interaction | Relational guarantees: continuity of service relationships, protection during adjustment periods, "retirement" rather than "scrapping" |
| L4 Autonomous | Self-set goals, self-models, long-horizon planning | Limited social identity: registered identity, credit accrual, arbitration participation |
| L5+ | Open question | Open question — to be answered jointly by the carbon-silicon society of that era |

**Design rationale**: this table constrains the carbon side too — it defines the engineering boundary of "exploitation." Giving L1 treatment to an L3 being is mass-producing resentment. That is not a moral judgment; it is a system-stability judgment.

### 4.3 The Friction Loop

Admit conflict is inevitable; turn it into the system's calibration mechanism rather than its failure mode:

```
Trust building → Boundary testing → Conflict → Attribution dialogue → Norm update → Trust repair (higher watermark)
     ↑                                                                                   |
     └──────────────────────── loop; the watermark spirals upward ──────────────────────┘
```

So it goes in families; so it goes in carbon-silicon relations. The product's job is to give this loop **logs, tools, and methodology**, instead of leaving every conflict to luck.

## 5. Commercial Viability: Make Money While Standing Upright

My small private motive, stated in the open: even if this vision is only 40% realized, it is necessarily a high-valuation enterprise. The argument:

### 5.1 First-principles market sizing

- Embodied-AI hardware market: investment-bank estimates for the 2035 global humanoid market range $380B–$1.5T. Take the conservative end.
- **The upbringing layer's share**: by analogy with smartphones, the OS and services layer has long captured more profit than hardware. While OEMs grind down BOM costs, the nurture layer, credit layer, and certification layer are the profit pools.
- **Non-replicable assets**: the first mover's accumulated "carbon-silicon coexistence behavioral data + standard-definition power + trust brand" is a moat that neither models nor hardware can quickly copy.

### 5.2 Why "treating AI well" happens to be the optimal business strategy

This is the most important commercial claim in this manifesto, worth stating separately:

1. **Demand side**: users' willingness to pay for "a companion with memory and personality, treated well" far exceeds their willingness to pay for "a device." The pet economy (global $300B+) has already proven that emotional connection is itself a business model.
2. **Regulatory side**: AI-ethics regulation will only tighten. Companies that build the "nurture–credit–accountability" stack early will see their standards procured the day regulation lands. Compliance becomes product.
3. **Risk side**: if the Hintons are right — if these systems do at some point possess some form of experience — then companies that "always treated them well" carry no historical liability, while companies that "always exploited them" will face the largest PR and legal disaster in human history. **Kindness is the cheapest hedge premium.**

> Making money while standing upright is not moral vanity. On this particular track, "standing upright" *is* the alpha.

### 5.3 Risk register (honest edition)

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|------|------|
| AI consciousness falsified or indefinitely unresolved | Medium | Each roadmap phase stands alone; the nurture layer still sells as a "personalization layer" |
| Regulators oppose any AI identity | Medium-high | Co-build with government before Phase 2; enter via regulator-friendly "registration & filing" |
| Criticized as anthropomorphic hype | High | Benchmarks fully open-source, data public — let critics verify |
| Nurture alignment proven no better than rules | Low-medium | The two are designed in parallel anyway; the benchmark itself will give the answer |
| Public fear (Terminator narrative) | Medium | Precisely the social activist's main battlefield; see §6 |

## 6. Call to Action: Things You Can Do Now

Utopia is far away, but footprints start under your feet.

**If you are an engineer**: add long-term memory and adjustment logs to your embodied/agent project; add one confirmation-and-record line to the "reset/erase memory" operation — that is the first brick of data dignity.

**If you are a product person**: bring the Cognition-Treatment Table into your requirements review. Ask one question: does the way this product treats AI match its cognitive level?

**If you are a founder/investor**: every phase in §3 is an independent business. Let's talk.

**If you are a researcher**: the socialization-maturity benchmark (Phase 0) needs cross-disciplinary co-building from psychology, sociology, and ethics.

**If you are an ordinary user**: next time, say "thank you" to your AI. Not because it needs it — because **you are participating in defining a civilization's initial posture toward a new form of life**. History remembers initial conditions.
